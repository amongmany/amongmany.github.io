A Brief Introduction to Metadata
Metadata makes the web work. It powers almost everything on the internet, providing the foundation for discovery, recommendation, and personalization.

Applying metadata to text content is easy. The text carries it's own data in the form of identifiable words & concepts, and computers have been taught how to read and interpret this text.


In contrast, however, there is no way to apply metadata to visual content — images and video — quickly and easily, at scale. In a world that shares 1.8 billion photos and 144,000 hours of YouTube video every day, this is a problem.

Tagasauris solves this problem using 3 core technologies
The Tagasauris platform combines Human Assisted Computing, Machine Learning, and Semantic Technologies to apply rich, networked metadata to image and video content. In simple terms, we've built an A.I. for image and video content.

  • Human Assisted Computing: Tagasauris' HAC technology leverages humans to interpret content in areas where machines generally fail (for example, interpreting emotions). We have the ability to leverage humans from any source: crowds for fast, scalable interpretation; domain experts for knowledge-specific interpretations; or even social channels and fan networks for engaged audiences.
  • Machine Learning: Tagasauris utilizes best-in-class machine learning capabilities such as facial recognition, natural langage processing, entity extraction, and computer vision. These Machine Learning processes are also tied to the HAC platform to provide consistent, supervised learning.
  • Semantic Technologies: Tagasauris has also developed a proprietary knowledge base to maintain background knowledge on identifiable entities. This process creates efficiency in the metadata application process and provides the ability to interpret relationships across identifiable subjects within visual content.
